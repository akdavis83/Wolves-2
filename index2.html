Wolf or Sheep?
Go your ways: behold, I send you forth as lambs among wolves. That means Jesus was sending them out to do the work of the ministry defenseless. The fullness of the power of the Holy Ghost had not yet been given to them and so in a measure they were helpless. They went out by themselves but the good Shepherd was near to protect them. Being lambs among wolves was also a word of caution about the carnal man. The carnal mind is hostile and at enmity with God and His word, way, and will. The natural man is doing just fine on his own, he does not need morals in his way. Jesus takes the idea one step further, wolves run in packs and some of the wolf pack on the surface look like real sheep. Once again their nature gives them away. You can hide a wolf but after a while when the carnivorous nature emerges, so too does the truth of what kind of creature you are dealing with. Two wolves might fight, two sheep might butt heads but they will not eat each other. Only when you mix the two species together the wolf will devour the sheep at long last.
